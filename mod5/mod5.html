Overivew

Web accessibility ensures that people with disabilities can perceive, understand, navigate, and interact with the web effectively. It is a legal requirement in many countries and a moral and ethical imperative. By making our websites accessible, we can provide equal access to information and services, promote inclusivity, and enhance the user experience for everyone.

Quiz

The assessment consists of 5 questions with a passing score set at 75%.

Assignment

you will apply the principles of web accessibility by conducting an audit of an existing website and creating an improvement plan to address accessibility issues. By doing so, you will not only gain a deeper understanding of web accessibility guidelines and standards, such as the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG), but also contribute to making the web a more inclusive and user-friendly environment for everyone.

Discussion

Imagine you are designing a website for a local community center. How would you apply the principles of user-centric design, accessibility, and navigation to create a website that meets the needs of its diverse users? Consider the different features, content organization, and design elements you would incorporate to make the website inclusive and user-friendly. Share your ideas and discuss with your classmates how these elements contribute to a better user experience.
