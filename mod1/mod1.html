Overivew

Web design principles encompass a range of key elements that contribute to a website's overall look, feel, and functionality. Layout involves organizing content in a logical and visually appealing manner, ensuring ease of navigation for users. Color plays a crucial role in setting the mood and tone of a website, as well as establishing brand identity. Typography involves selecting and styling fonts to enhance readability and effectively convey the website's message. User experience focuses on creating a seamless and enjoyable interaction between the user and the website, aiming to meet the user's needs and expectations. All these elements work together to create a cohesive and engaging web design that effectively communicates the intended message to the audience.

Lesson

Quiz

The assessment consists of 10 questions with a passing score set at 75%.

Assignment

Provide a case study of a poorly designed website and ask groups to analyze its design principles (or lack thereof). Groups can then redesign the website, applying the principles discussed in the module, and present their redesigned version and a rationale for their design choices.

Discussion

Discuss a website you find visually appealing. What design elements (layout, color, typography) contribute to its appeal? How do these elements enhance the user experience?
